Vegan Banh Mi

Description

Beautiful, crusty baguettes filled with fried tofu, pickled vegetables, spicy mayo, and seasoned with fresh cilantro.

Banh Mi is a Vietnamese sandwich, typically filled with pork, but with this vegan version, you won't even miss the meat!

The crunchy and sour vegetables with the crispy tofu and rich mayo create a delicious sandwich that you will definitely crave all the time!

Ingredients

  • 1 package extra firm tofu
  • Olive oil
  • 4 baguette pieces
  • Sriracha and mayonnaise for the spicy mayo
  • Fresh cilantro to garnsih
  • 1 small daikon, julienned
  • 2 small carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 a cucumber, julienned
  • about 1/3 cup rice wine vinegar to pickle
  • Sugar and salt as needed
  • Tamari or soy sauce, for the tofu marinade
  • 1/2 a lime for the marinade
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp of ginger, minced

Steps

  1. Pickle the vegetables ahead of time: Place the julienned vegetables in a jar with water, vinegar, salt and sugar and let chill for at least an hour or up to a week
  2. Drain the tofu, squeezing out excess liquid, and cut into 1/2 inch pieces and let dry on a paper towel
  3. Whisk together tamari, olive oil, lime juice + zest, ginger and garlic to create the marinade
  4. Coat the tofu with the marinade and let it marinate for at least 15 minutes
  5. In a nonstick skillet on medium-high heat, pan-fry the tofu on each side till golden brown
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together mayo and sriracha
  7. Assemble the sandwiches with the mayo, pickled vegetables and tofu and enjoy!
